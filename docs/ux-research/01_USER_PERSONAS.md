# User Personas
## Dumuwaks Service Marketplace

---

## Overview

Three primary user personas have been identified for Dumuwaks. Each represents a distinct user type with unique goals, pain points, and contexts of use.

---

## Persona 1: The Customer - "Wanjiku the Homeowner"

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Wanjiku Muthoni |
| **Age** | 34 years old |
| **Location** | Kilimani, Nairobi |
| **Occupation** | Marketing Manager at a tech company |
| **Income** | KES 150,000 - 200,000/month |
| **Education** | Bachelor's degree |
| **Tech Literacy** | High (uses smartphone for everything) |
| **Primary Device** | iPhone 13 / Samsung S23 (375px width) |
| **Secondary Device** | Work laptop |
| **Connectivity** | 4G + home WiFi |
| **Payment Methods** | M-Pesa, Credit Card, Bank Transfer |

### Context of Use

- **When:** Evenings (7-9 PM) after work, weekends
- **Where:** Home, sometimes at work during lunch
- **Posture:** Relaxed, on couch or bed
- **Attention Level:** Distracted (TV in background, kids around)
- **Time Available:** 5-15 minutes per session
- **One-Handed Operation:** 70% of the time

### Goals

#### Primary Goals
1. **Find a qualified technician quickly** - "I need someone to fix my leaking tap TODAY"
2. **Trust the person coming to my home** - "Are they verified? What do others say?"
3. **Know the cost upfront** - "How much will this cost me before they arrive?"
4. **Book without hassle** - "I don't want to make 5 phone calls"

#### Secondary Goals
1. Compare multiple technicians
2. See examples of previous work
3. Pay conveniently (M-Pesa preferred)
4. Communicate directly with technician
5. Leave reviews to help others

### Pain Points

#### Physical Constraints
- Small phone screen makes reading lots of text difficult
- Poor network in some areas of Nairobi
- Battery anxiety when out and about

#### Environmental Factors
- Distractions at home (children, TV, household chores)
- Time pressure (needs quick solutions)
- Noise in public spaces

#### Cognitive Load Factors
- **Information overload:** Too many technicians to compare
- **Decision paralysis:** "Which one should I choose?"
- **Trust uncertainty:** "Can I trust this stranger in my home?"
- **Price ambiguity:** "Will there be hidden costs?"

### Frustrations with Current Solutions

1. **"I have to describe my problem in words, but I don't know technical terms"**
   - Wants to show a photo and say "fix this"
   - Does not know if it is a "ball valve" or "washer" issue

2. **"I call 5 technicians and they all give different prices"**
   - Wants transparent, standardized pricing
   - Tired of negotiation

3. **"I cannot tell if they are good until they arrive"**
   - Wants to see real photos of past work
   - Wants verified reviews from real people

4. **"The booking process takes too long"**
   - Wants to tap a few times and be done
   - Does not want to fill long forms

5. **"I cannot reach them when I need them"**
   - Technicians do not pick calls
   - No way to know if they are available

### Success Criteria

| Scenario | Success Metric |
|----------|----------------|
| Emergency leak | Technician arrives within 2 hours |
| Planned repair | Booking completed in under 5 minutes |
| Price inquiry | Clear estimate before booking |
| Quality assurance | Work matches gallery examples |

### Quote

> "I just want to take a photo of my problem, see who can fix it, how much it will cost, and book them. Why is this so complicated?"

## Persona 2: The Technician - "Kamau the Plumber"

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Peter Kamau Ngugi |
| **Age** | 42 years old |
| **Location** | Kayole, Nairobi |
| **Business Location** | Operates within 15km radius |
| **Occupation** | Self-employed plumber (10 years experience) |
| **Income** | KES 40,000 - 80,000/month (variable) |
| **Education** | Form 4, Trade Certificate in Plumbing |
| **Tech Literacy** | Medium (uses WhatsApp, M-Pesa daily) |
| **Primary Device** | Tecno Spark 10 (360px width, Android) |
| **Secondary Device** | None |
| **Connectivity** | 3G/4G (inconsistent in some areas) |
| **Payment Methods** | M-Pesa (essential), Cash |

### Context of Use

- **When:** Early morning (6-7 AM), lunch breaks, evenings (after jobs)
- **Where:** On the road, at job sites, at home
- **Posture:** Standing or walking, often one-handed
- **Attention Level:** Split between app and physical work
- **Time Available:** 2-5 minutes per check
- **One-Handed Operation:** 90% of the time (tools in other hand)

### Goals

#### Primary Goals
1. **Get consistent bookings** - "I need jobs every day to feed my family"
2. **Showcase my work** - "Let customers see what I can do"
3. **Get paid reliably** - "No more 'I will pay you tomorrow'"
4. **Build reputation** - "I want to be the go-to plumber in my area"

#### Secondary Goals
1. Define my own services and prices
2. Communicate easily with customers
3. Manage my schedule
4. Minimize travel time between jobs
5. Upsell additional services

### Pain Points

#### Physical Constraints
- Budget smartphone with small screen
- Cracks on screen make reading difficult
- Low storage (cannot store many photos)
- Poor battery life (needs to last all day)

#### Environmental Factors
- Working outdoors in sun (screen glare)
- Dusty and wet conditions
- Noise at job sites
- Intermittent network in basements and remote areas

#### Cognitive Load Factors
- **Language barriers:** English forms are difficult
- **Form fatigue:** Long registration/profile forms
- **Technology anxiety:** Fear of "doing it wrong"
- **Time pressure:** Cannot spend 30 minutes setting up profile

### Frustrations with Current Solutions

1. **"The categories do not match what I actually do"**
   - Wants to add services like "bathroom renovation" or "water tank installation"
   - Feels limited by predefined options

2. **"Customers think I am expensive before they see my work"**
   - Wants to show quality before price discussion
   - Gallery limit (5 photos) is not enough

3. **"I travel far for a job and the customer is not there"**
   - Wants better communication before traveling
   - Needs deposit/booking fee commitment

4. **"I do not get paid sometimes"**
   - Customers cancel after work is done
   - Wants payment guarantee

5. **"How do I stand out from other plumbers?"**
   - Wants visibility boost
   - Needs differentiation beyond price

### Service Definition Needs

Kamau wants to offer:

| Service | His Price | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Tap repair | KES 500-1,500 | Depending on parts |
| Toilet installation | KES 3,000-5,000 | Labor only |
| Water tank installation | KES 2,500-4,000 | Excludes tank |
| Bathroom renovation | KES 15,000+ | Depends on scope |
| Emergency leak repair | KES 1,000-3,000 | Within 2 hours |
| Pipe unblocking | KES 800-2,000 | Depending on severity |
| Water heater installation | KES 2,000-3,500 | Labor only |

### Success Criteria

| Scenario | Success Metric |
|----------|----------------|
| Daily bookings | 2-4 jobs per day |
| Payment reliability | 100% payment within 24 hours |
| Profile completion | Gallery + services + pricing complete |
| Customer satisfaction | 4.5+ star rating |

### Quote

> "I have been a plumber for 10 years. I just want to show customers my work, tell them what I charge, and get booked. I do not want to fill 20 forms."

## Persona 3: The Admin - "Atieno the Operations Manager"

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Grace Atieno Ochieng |
| **Age** | 38 years old |
| **Location** | Westlands, Nairobi (Office) |
| **Occupation** | Operations Manager at Dumuwaks |
| **Education** | MBA, Operations Management |
| **Tech Literacy** | Very High |
| **Primary Device** | MacBook Pro + iPhone 14 |
| **Work Environment** | Office desk, dual monitors |
| **Connectivity** | Fiber internet + 4G backup |

### Context of Use

- **When:** Work hours (8 AM - 6 PM), sometimes weekends
- **Where:** Office desk
- **Posture:** Seated, focused
- **Attention Level:** High (dedicated work time)
- **Time Available:** Extended sessions (1-4 hours)

### Goals

#### Primary Goals
1. **Monitor platform health** - "Are bookings flowing? Any issues?"
2. **Ensure quality control** - "Are technicians delivering good service?"
3. **Handle disputes** - "Resolve conflicts fairly and quickly"
4. **Grow the business** - "More customers, more technicians, more revenue"

#### Secondary Goals
1. Verify technician credentials
2. Analyze booking patterns and trends
3. Manage payouts to technicians
4. Handle customer complaints
5. Generate reports for stakeholders

### Pain Points

#### System Complexity
- Multiple dashboards to check (Admin, Reports, Support, User Management)
- Data scattered across different views
- No unified "command center"

#### Operational Challenges
- Difficulty spotting problems early
- Manual verification processes are slow
- Hard to identify fraudulent activity

#### Decision-Making
- Limited real-time insights
- Data not actionable enough
- Too many raw metrics, not enough meaning

### Success Criteria

| Scenario | Success Metric |
|----------|----------------|
| Issue resolution | < 24 hours to resolve disputes |
| Platform monitoring | Real-time visibility into all operations |
| Decision-making | Data available in < 3 clicks |
| Fraud prevention | < 1% fraudulent transactions |

### Quote

> "I need to see everything at a glance. When there is a problem, I need to know immediately and have the tools to fix it."

## Key Takeaways for Design

### For Customers (Wanjiku)
1. **Visual-first service discovery** - Let photos speak, minimize text
2. **Quick booking** - Under 5 minutes, under 10 taps
3. **Trust signals** - Show verifications, reviews, real photos prominently
4. **Transparent pricing** - Show cost ranges upfront
5. **Photo-based problem description** - Let her show, not tell

### For Technicians (Kamau)
1. **Custom services** - Let him define what he offers and at what price
2. **Easy gallery** - Simple photo upload, more than 5 slots
3. **Swahili-friendly** - Interface in Swahili or simple English
4. **Offline-capable** - Works in basements and areas with poor network
5. **One-handed operation** - Large touch targets, simple navigation

### For Admins (Atieno)
1. **Unified dashboard** - Everything in one place
2. **Real-time monitoring** - Live updates on platform health
3. **Actionable data** - Insights, not just raw numbers
4. **Quick resolution tools** - Fast dispute handling
5. **Fraud detection** - Automatic flagging of suspicious activity
