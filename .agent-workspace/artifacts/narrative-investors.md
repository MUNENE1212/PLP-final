## STORY Framework: The Narrative Flow

### S - SETTING THE STAGE

**The African Context:**

Imagine you're a homeowner in Nairobi. Your kitchen sink starts leaking at 10pm on a Tuesday. Water is spreading across your floor. You need help NOW.

What do you do?

You call the plumber whose number you have from a job two years ago. No answer. You call your neighbor - they don't have anyone. You post on Facebook: "Anyone know a good plumber?" Crickets. You search Google - find classifieds with phone numbers from 2019. You call five numbers. Three don't answer, one says "come tomorrow," one quotes you 8,000 KES but won't guarantee when they'll arrive.

It's now 11pm. Your floor is wet. You're stressed. You're thinking: "There has to be a better way."

**The Technician Reality:**

Now imagine you're a skilled plumber. You've got 8 years of experience. You're a Master plumber with certifications. You do great work. But you're sitting at home because you don't have any jobs today.

Why? Because customers can't find you. They call the same three plumbers everyone knows - the ones with big marketing budgets or who happen to be neighbors with influential people. You're better than them, but you're invisible.

You spend 3-4 hours every day calling people, posting on Facebook groups, asking hardware store owners for referrals. That's unpaid time. When you DO get a job, the customer bargains you down: "Can you do it for 3,000 KES? My cousin said he'd do it for 2,000." You take it because you need the money, even though it's not fair for your expertise.

Some weeks you make 45,000 KES. Some weeks you make 8,000 KES. You can't plan. You can't save. You can't grow.

**The Market Scale:**

This isn't one story. This is MILLIONS of stories across Kenya, across Africa.

- **$150B+ informal services sector** across Africa
- **Kenya alone: $25-30B maintenance/repair market**
- **70%+ of urban Africans** report negative experiences finding reliable service providers
- **40-60% of technicians' time** wasted on unpaid activities (finding customers, negotiating)

This is the LARGEST inefficiency in Africa's economy. And it's COMPLETELY UNSERVED by technology.

**The Current Solutions Don't Work:**

- **Classifieds (Jiji, etc.):** No trust, no verification, no payment protection - just digital bulletin boards
- **Word-of-Mouth:** Limited, inefficient, non-scalable
- **Directories:** Static listings, no activity tracking, no reputation systems

The market is BEGGING for a solution that actually works.
